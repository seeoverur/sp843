Exploring the finest artisan cafes and unique brews along **King Street** and the historic district of **Charleston, SC**.

Top 8 Coffee Shops in Downtown Charleston

1. Sweet Palm Coffee Official Website Google Profile

A local favorite known for its signature lattes and robust espresso program. Sweet Palm balances classic coffee craft with creative Southern-inspired flavors, offering both quality brews and light café food. It’s a comfortable spot right off the King Street buzz.

2. Clerks Coffee Company

Located within The Dewberry hotel, Clerks offers an elevated, bright atmosphere and skillfully prepared pour-overs. Perfect for a refined morning start or a quiet afternoon of work, embodying Southern hospitality.

3. Black Tap Coffee

Known for their dedication to meticulously brewed single-origin coffees and cold brew. This small shop is a favorite among students and locals for its unpretentious, high-quality focus on the bean itself.

4. Brown's Court Bakery

More than just coffee, Brown’s Court offers a full selection of freshly baked bread, pastries, and incredible breakfast sandwiches alongside reliable coffee staples. Its courtyard seating is a hidden downtown gem.

5. Kudu Coffee & Craft Beer

Famous for its large, shady outdoor patio and dual identity as a great daytime coffee spot and an evening craft beer destination. It provides a laid-back, community-focused environment slightly off the main King Street drag.

6. Second State Coffee

A roasting company with a strong commitment to sustainable practices. They provide clean, bright flavors in their espresso and offer an airy space perfect for studying or meeting friends.

7. The Daily

While also offering a full menu of provisions, The Daily is renowned for its excellent counter-service coffee program, featuring unique seasonal drinks and high-quality beans in a modern, stylish setting.

8. Bitty & Beau's Coffee

A socially conscious café focused on employing people with intellectual and developmental disabilities. They serve great coffee and provide an incredibly heartwarming and welcoming atmosphere for all patrons on Wentworth Street.
